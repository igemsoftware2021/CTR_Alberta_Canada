Sustainable Facilities

Sustainable Hotels and Restaurants

Element

Element is a corporation of sustainable design consultants, dedicated to aiding clients on sustainable concepts for buildings in Canada, Alberta. Element collaboratively works with clients and project managers to analyze the proposed design and construction plans that comply with the Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) rating system. Element is able to identify energy minimization and optimization strategies to support the design of mechanical and electrical systems.

Intercontinental

All Intercontinental hotels use the IHG Green Engage system, an innovative online environmental sustainability system that allows their hotels the means to measure and manage their impact on the environment. Intercontinental has achieved a Level 3 certification according to the IHG Green Engage System, reducing their energy use by 25%. Level 3 certification includes installing energy-efficient appliances and sustainable site management, alongside Level 1 and Level 2 certifications, which include tracking consumption data, installing energy-efficient lighting, and sustainable purchasing.

Hilton

Hilton Hotels and Resorts are committed to protecting and preserving the destinations that they operate in. Through the LightStay system, Hilton measures, reports and improves the environmental impact across managed and franchised hotels. Hilton is the first major hotel company that has science-based carbon targets that align with the Paris Climate Agreement. Hilton also plans to implement 20 context-based water stewardship pilots in areas on top of water risk, alongside waste reduction through diverting waste through donating, recycling, and composting.

Shake Shack

The Shake Shack is an American fast-food and restaurant fusion chain that is known for its burgers and fries. Shake Shack burgers are 100% Angus beef, sourced from humanely raised, source verified, and vegetarian fed cows. Each restaurant location is constructed using reclaimed barn wood, and they are currently implementing environmentally friendly cutlery and straws for “to-go” orders and aluminium water bottles. The cutlery and straws contain no synthetic plastic, PLA or synthetic glues, making them fully compostable.

Copper Branch

Copper Branch serves 100% quality, plant-based food. Their organic and non-GMO ingredients aim to serve customers with “real nutrition”. Copper Branch is dedicated to sustainability, as they search, and adopt, vegan, cruelty-free practices to further heighten the standards of the food industry. Through the exclusion of animal products, Copper Branch contributes to preventing climate change, deforestation, excessive water use, species extinction, waste production, and land use.

Chipotle

Chipotle is a Mexican inspired casual restaurant food chain, known for its burritos and rice bowls. Chipotle had recently implemented tier Environmental, Social and Governance, (ESG) metric ties, by using ten percent of officers’ annual incentive bonuses. The objectives hold the executive leadership team responsible for making business decisions that cultivate a better world.
